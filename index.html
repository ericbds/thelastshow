The Premise

A job that no longer exists.

It is 1951. You are a substitute projectionist covering a late-night screening at a dying independent cinema. Thread the reels. Execute the changeovers. Keep the audience from walking out.

The horror does not chase you. It uses you. Something has found its way into the archive reels — and the only way to bring the seats back is to run the film anyway, knowing what it costs each time the projector light hits those frames.

Every decision is a trade. Every trade has a price.

The Tone

Craft, dread, and something true.

The booth is worth inhabiting. The equipment is real, the job is real, and the world it belongs to is gone. There is something melancholy about that — and something deeply satisfying about doing it right.

The horror layers on top of a job that is already stressful. You are never fighting it. You are trying to keep working while it makes that harder. The distinction is the game.

Core Systems

The job is the game.

01

The Changeover

Watch for the cigarette-burn cue mark through the port window. Thread the next reel. Pull the lever at the right moment. Miss it and the movie stops. The audience notices.

02

Reel Preparation

Inspect every reel on the editing bench before it runs. Find splice damage. Find cursed frames. Decide what to cut and what to keep — before the projector makes that choice for you.

03

The Cursed Archive

Some reels carry something wrong inside them. Project those frames and the empty seats start to fill — but the haunting climbs with every second the light runs through them. The only healing item in the game is also the worst thing you can do.

04

Heat Management

The projectors run hot. The fan slows the rise. Watch the thermometer. The longer a reel runs without the fan, the closer you are to a film burn — and the monster knows exactly when to turn it off.

05

The Audience

The seats below you are not a number. You hear them — murmuring, shuffling, walking out. Every viewer who leaves is gone for good. The end report tells you exactly how many you kept and why you lost the rest.

06

The Haunting

It starts as atmosphere. Knocks. Flickers. A shadow in the hallway you almost saw. The more cursed film you project, the more real it becomes. The haunting never resets. It only climbs.

The Structure

Four reels. One night.

The theatre closes when the final reel runs out. The outcome is determined by who is still sitting in the seats when the lights come up.

--:--

11pm — Reel 1

The screening begins. Learn the booth. Find the rhythm. The first reel is never forgiving.

Midnight — Reel 2

Something shifts. Equipment failures compound. The archive starts revealing itself.

1am — Reel 3

Interference is constant. The haunting is real now. Three problems at once, every time.

2am — Reel 4

Maximum pressure. Final changeover. Whatever is left in the seats when it ends — that's your score.
