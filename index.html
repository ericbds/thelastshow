The Premise

A job that no longer exists.

Operate a 35mm carbon arc projector in a dying independent cinema. Thread the reels. Execute the changeovers. Manage failing equipment. Keep the audience from walking out.

The horror is layered on top of a job that is already stressful. The monster does not chase you — he sabotages the equipment at the worst possible moment. You are never fighting the horror. You are trying to do your job while the horror makes that harder.

That distinction is the game.

The Tone

Dread and dark humour.

An audience member delivering a full animated exit gesture on the way out because the mood dropped too low is funny and humiliating simultaneously.

Your character screaming at a jumpscare, then shakily lighting a cigarette, is human and specific. The tone lives exactly where the best horror has always lived.

Core Systems

The job is the game.

01

The Changeover

Read the cigarette-burn cue marks through the port window. Thread the next reel. Pull the lever at exactly the right moment. Miss it and the movie stops. The audience notices.

02

Heat Management

The projectors run hot. The fan slows the rise. The thermometer tells you how close to a film burn you are. The monster accelerates it when you least need him to.

03

The Audience

Three hundred people below you. No UI. No meter. You read their mood through sound — murmuring, shuffling, complaints, footsteps toward the exit. Every walkout is permanent.

04

The Monster

He doesn't chase you. He watches. He interferes. He trips the breaker while the projector is already overheating. He plays the game against you. His goal is to empty the cinema.

05

Splice Repair

Bad reels break mid-show. The splicer bench is in the booth. Trim, align, seal. Do it fast enough and the audience barely notices. Do it slow and you'll hear the consequences.

06

The Modifier System

Every endless run draws from a pool of 30 modifiers. Unmarked Reels. No Flashlight. Early Arrival. Sold Out. No two nights are the same. Every run is a story worth telling.

The Structure

Every run ends at 3am.

The theatre closes. The final reel ends. Whatever has been in the building reaches its peak and stops.
You are not trying to defeat it. You are trying to outlast it.

3:00

11pm — Reel 1

The screening begins. Equipment old but holding. Learn the job. Find the rhythm.

Midnight — Reel 2

Something shifts. Failures become more frequent. The first disruptions appear.

1am — Reel 3

Monster events begin in earnest. Three simultaneous concerns. Everything at once.

2am — Reel 4

Maximum pressure. Final changeover under the worst conditions of the night. Survive until 3.

Spiritual References

Where it comes from.

Hitchcock's Psycho for atmosphere. Papers Please for mechanical philosophy. Balatro for replayability design. Phasmophobia for proof that atmospheric horror built on equipment interaction can find a massive audience.
